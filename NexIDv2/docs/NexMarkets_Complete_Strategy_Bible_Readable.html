01. Why This Exists

Prediction markets are powerful, but mainstream users rarely think in order books, CLOB tokens, maker/taker language, or resolution mechanics. They think in moments: a token rotation, a transfer rumor, an award race, a football match, a culture fight, a startup launch, a celebrity/public-event outcome. NexMarkets makes those moments tradable.

Core painPeople have strong theses before markets exist. Existing platforms are curated, slow to list long-tail narratives, and often too technical for casual audiences.
Core promiseType what you believe. NexMarkets finds the existing market or helps you launch one with clean rules.

The wedge

  • Crypto / CT: fast narratives, strong appetite for public calls, creator/KOL distribution, and wallet familiarity.
  • Football / Sports: repeated events, tribal conviction, natural prediction behavior, and mainstream volume potential.
  • Culture / Entertainment: viral debates, chart races, awards, reality TV, public drama and receipts people want to share.

Why not only Polymarket?

Polymarket is powerful as the existing liquidity layer. But Polymarket market creation is curated; users can suggest markets, but they do not directly launch them. NexMarkets solves the long-tail gap by routing what exists and enabling native creation where no clean market exists.

04. User Flows

A. Guest sees a market and trades existing route

1
User opens Pulse.
They see “HYPE $50 run” as a live market card. The backend has matched it to Polymarket.
2
User chooses Ride or Fade.
Ride means the underlying question resolves true. Fade means it does not.
3
Trade routes through Polymarket.
NexMarkets uses builder attribution where supported. The user sees a simple market room, not technical rails.
4
Receipt forms.
The position creates a timestamped proof. Full passport benefits require .id.

B. Creator launches missing native market

  1. User clicks Launch or types a thesis from Pulse.
  2. AI Market Composer shapes the thesis into a market draft.
  3. Route check runs against Polymarket and native markets.
  4. If exact existing market is found, user is sent to trade/join it.
  5. If no exact match and market is allowed, user pays $20 launch stake.
  6. Market enters live-pending-open cooldown.
  7. Trading opens after cooldown with early controls.
  8. Creator earns 1% of native trading volume and gets $10 bond back if market settles cleanly.

C. .id user builds reputation

A user with .id carries every call, creator launch, receipt, dispute history, referral and EdgeBoard rank under one portable identity. The .id is not cosmetic. It is the trust object for a permissionless market system.

05. Market System

Three market origins

Polymarket routeEquivalent existing liquid market exists.Narrative market card with Ride/Fade.Order routes via Polymarket APIs/builder attribution.
Native NexMarketNo exact Polymarket market exists and market is allowed.User-launched market with creator .id and $20 launch stake.Base smart contracts, USDC collateral, bonding curve, resolution flow.

Market matching hierarchy

  1. Exact native duplicate: block launch and send to existing NexMarket.
  2. Exact Polymarket match: display as NexMarkets narrative card and route trading.
  3. Related Polymarket match: show the existing market and allow a legitimate variant if timeframe/metric/source differs.
  4. No clean match: allow native launch if template, source and risk checks pass.

07. Native Engine

Why native markets use bonding curves

New long-tail narratives cannot rely on empty order books. A bonding-curve AMM gives every allowed native market instant tradability. It is not intended to replace Polymarket CLOB liquidity. It is for markets Polymarket does not list.

Native market primitives

  • USDC collateral on Base.
  • Ride/Fade outcome shares minted/bought through curve.
  • Market creator identified by .id.
  • Locked rules hash generated at launch.
  • Cooldown before trading opens.
  • Early exposure caps to reduce creator/whale manipulation.
  • Resolution through objective source and optimistic dispute mechanism.

08. Resolution + Disputes

NexMarkets must not be the central judge. The correct mechanism is optimistic: rules are locked at launch, an outcome is proposed after close, and anyone can dispute within a defined window. This mirrors the general pattern used by UMA and by Polymarket’s own resolution model.

Resolution flow

  1. Market closes.
  2. Resolver bot or any eligible proposer submits outcome with evidence.
  3. Proposer posts bond.
  4. Dispute window opens.
  5. If no dispute, outcome finalizes.
  6. If disputed, disputer posts bond and dispute escalates to oracle/adjudication mechanism.
  7. Final result settles winners, receipts, creator bond and EdgeBoard.

Invalid market handling

Invalid reasonOutcome
Rules impossible to resolveRefund if possible, slash creator quality bond, no points.
Duplicate native marketBlock before launch when detected; if detected later and bad-faith, slash bond.
Blocked unsafe categoryBlock before launch. If bypassed, cancel/invalid according to protocol rules.
Source unavailable with no fallbackDispute process determines refund/invalid or fallback use.

Important distinction

Low volume is not invalid. If a market has clear rules and can resolve, it settles even if volume is low. Volume only affects ranking, rewards, and featured placement.

09. Anti-Manipulation

The product must stay exciting, but native markets cannot be a free-for-all. Controls should be mostly invisible to normal users and stricter in the first minutes after launch.

10. AI Market Composer

No conversational agent. The product uses a fast Thesis Studio: the user types what they believe, and AI shapes it into a clean market card plus locked rules.

AI jobs

  1. Parse thesis into arena, entities, metric, timeframe and likely market type.
  2. Check existing Polymarket and native markets.
  3. Transform vague language into precise rules.
  4. Ask only necessary refinements with chips, not a long chat.
  5. Flag blocked/ambiguous markets.
  6. Generate receipt/share copy after launch.

15. Polymarket Routing

Polymarket is the existing liquidity bridge. NexMarkets should restructure market presentation in narrative language but never alter the settlement reality. If an order routes to Polymarket, the market room must expose what the actual underlying question settles on.

Mapping rules

  • Exact match: display as NexMarkets market and route orders.
  • Related but different: show it as related; allow native launch only if timeframe/metric/source differs materially.
  • Weak keyword match: do not route as equivalent.
  • No match: user can launch native if allowed.

Builder integration

Use Polymarket CLOB/Relayer clients and builder attribution where available. Engineering must verify current docs, supported assets, fees, relayer limits, geoblocking and builder requirements before production.

Security rule: Do not launch uncapped real-money native markets without independent security review. Start with Polymarket routing, drafts, testnet, then capped mainnet canary.

ResolutionManagerOutcome proposal, dispute window, oracle integration, settlement finalization.
PassportRegistryRead/verify .id ownership or integrate with deployed .id contract.
EmergencyGuardPause/caps/canary limits with timelock and transparent controls.

Canary caps

  • Max native protocol TVL.
  • Max per-market volume.
  • Max per-wallet exposure.
  • Only approved templates.
  • Limited categories at first.
  • Bug bounty and monitoring active.

Keep immutable records for launch drafts, final rules, market state transitions, proposals, disputes, receipts, and point events. These records are the trust layer.

21. Operations

Daily operations

  • Review flagged ambiguous launches.
  • Monitor native market caps, abnormal volume and failed transactions.
  • Resolve customer issues around wallets, pings, receipts and .id.
  • Publish top markets and receipts for content loops.
  • Track market categories that create volume vs spam.

Launch operations

  1. Public brand + waitlist + .id early access.
  2. Polymarket route-only MVP.
  3. Market draft studio with no native funds.
  4. Public Base testnet native markets.
  5. Capped mainnet canary.
  6. Independent audit + bug bounty expansion.
  7. Uncapped only after security milestones.

Support policies

  • Make market rules visible before trade.
  • Show settlement source on every market room.
  • Never promise liquidity.
  • Never imply AI predicts outcomes.
  • Keep blocked categories strict.

22. Security + Audit

Audit reality

If you cannot afford a full audit, do not launch uncapped native real-money markets. Use staged release: route-only, drafts, testnet, capped canary, then audit.

Security ladder

StageWhat shipsRisk control
0Polymarket routing + draftsNo native custody risk.
1Base testnet native marketsFake USDC, public testing.
2Capped mainnet canaryTVL/trade caps, limited templates, monitoring, pause.
3Independent reviewFocused audit of factory, curve, stake, fee, resolution.
4Expanded launchBug bounty and broader templates.

Tools

  • Foundry unit/fuzz/invariant tests.
  • Slither static analysis.
  • OpenZeppelin libraries for access control and safety primitives.
  • OpenZeppelin Defender Monitor for runtime alerts.
  • Independent auditor or competitive review when funds allow.

26. QA

Product QA

  • Ride/Fade only appears when a market is actually tradable.
  • Launch Market appears only when no exact duplicate exists and market is allowed.
  • Ambiguous markets force refinement.
  • Blocked markets cannot be launched.
  • Every market room shows settlement source and rules.
  • Every receipt has timestamp, market ID, side and .id/wallet attribution.
  • Every point maps to a receipt or ledger event.

Engineering QA

  • Unit tests for all contract modules.
  • Fuzz tests for launch stake, fee split, bonding curve buy/sell/redeem.
  • Invariant tests: collateral safety, no over-redemption, fee totals, bond state.
  • Static analysis: Slither and dependency checks.
  • Route matching tests across exact/related/duplicate examples.
  • AI schema tests: no invalid enums, no missing fields.
  • Frontend tests: Pulse, Launch, Market, Passport, EdgeBoard, Profile, theme.
  • Monitoring alerts: abnormal volume, failed settlements, pause triggers.
