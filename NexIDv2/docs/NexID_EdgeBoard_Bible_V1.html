1. Product thesis

NexID EdgeBoard turns a real CT behavior into a product: users take sides on narratives, risk capital, flex outcomes, compete for status, and build a public history of edge.

The wedge: people do not need another abstract reputation score. They need proof that they caught, rode, faded, or survived a narrative before the crowd did.
Not a generic reputation appReputation emerges from positions, receipts, board performance and proof quality.
Not a raw market terminalRaw markets are translated into CT-native Ride/Fade narrative choices.
Not domain-first.id appears after users have something worth owning: receipts, rankings, referrals and identity.

2. What NexID is

NexID EdgeBoard is a CT-native narrative edge product. Users discover live narratives, take a Ride/Fade side, generate position cards and receipts, earn Edge Points, rank on EdgeBoards, qualify for recurring .id Rewards, and turn their history into a portable .id edge passport.

LayerWhat it doesWhy it matters
Public narrativesGroups raw markets into CT-native themes.Users understand “AI Agents” faster than isolated market questions.
Ride/FadeConverts a market into a simple side.Action feels like CT language, not market jargon.
ReceiptsTurns positions and outcomes into shareable proof.Creates the viral artifact.
EdgeBoardsRanks users by edge, points, receipts and categories.Creates status competition.
.idClaims the user’s edge history as a portable identity.Creates monetization and long-term reputation.

3. Audience & CT culture

The first audience is crypto-native CT users who already understand narrative trading, PnL screenshots, leaderboard status, points, referral games and identity flexing.

Primary userCT trader, degen, narrative scout, KOL, ecosystem participant, prediction-market user, small account trying to prove edge.
Secondary userProtocol team, ecosystem team, market watcher, community operator, high-signal regional user, future API consumer.

Why this can feel native

  • CT already trades narratives.
  • CT already argues about who was early.
  • CT already posts PnL screenshots and receipts.
  • CT already understands points, seasons, referrals and leaderboards.
  • CT understands risk and status better than abstract “reputation.”

5. Full user experience

Homepage

The homepage must feel like the front page of what CT is trading now. It should not be a long SaaS explanation. It should show live narratives, movement, top receipts, EdgeBoard snapshots and a clear action: Explore live narratives or Take a side.

Live narratives

Users browse narrative cards showing heat, movement, liquidity, spread, riders, faders, top receipt and a Ride/Fade CTA.

Narrative detail

This is the trading arena: chart, liquidity, spread, market quality, riders/faders, comments, receipts, rules and a sticky Ride/Fade ticket. The sticky ticket only belongs here, not everywhere.

Take-a-side ticket

Users select Ride or Fade, choose Market or Limit, enter amount, review max loss/max return/fees/slippage/expiry, then confirm position.

After position

Immediately show a position card, pending points, and live dashboard state. After closure/resolution, generate receipt card and update boards.

7. Market mapping engine

Batch 2 exists to turn raw markets into NexID narratives. It should not create a new public product screen. It powers the existing Live Narratives and Narrative Detail pages.

Pipeline

Gamma/Data/CLOB reads
  → raw market normalizer
  → tags/categories fetcher
  → narrative matching engine
  → market quality score
  → cache layer
  → public narrative API
  → existing Batch 1 UI

Market Quality Score

Not every market is tradable in NexID. Quality filters protect the user experience.

SignalPurpose
LiquidityAvoid dead or thin markets.
SpreadAvoid punishing users with poor entry.
VolumeConfirm market is alive.
ExpiryEnsure timeframe makes sense for the narrative.
Resolution clarityReduce ambiguous outcomes.
Orderbook enabledOnly tradable markets should surface Ride/Fade CTAs.
Location eligibilityPrevent users from reaching a blocked order flow.
Category fitEnsure market actually expresses the narrative.

No-trade fallback

If no market passes quality checks, the UI should show: No clean position available yet. Watch this narrative. Users can still follow the narrative, view comments, and earn lighter engagement points later, but cannot place a position.

8. Ride/Fade execution

NexID should abstract order complexity into Ride/Fade language while respecting the underlying order mechanics. The user sees “Ride AI Agents” or “Fade AI Agents”; the system maps this to outcome tokens and order side.

Trading states

Pre-orderNot connected, connected, restricted location, wrong chain, market closed, no trade available.
Order entrySide selected, amount entered, market/limit toggle, price moved, spread warning, insufficient balance.
Post-orderConfirming, placed, failed, partial fill, filled, live, closed, resolved.

Under the hood

  • Public market data is fetched through public read endpoints.
  • Trading actions require authenticated order-management flow.
  • Market-style orders should use immediate execution types where supported.
  • Limit orders should rest on the book and show clear expiry/cancel behavior.
  • NexID attaches builder attribution where supported and eligible.
Product rule: Never make the user feel they left NexID. If an external rail powers the trade, it should still feel like a NexID position.

10. Edge Points

Edge Points are the seasonal progression layer. They unlock reward levels and badges, while the weekly reward score determines that cycle's share of the loyalty pool. Main UI should feel confident and competitive; deeper anti-abuse wording lives in Points and Rewards Terms.

Earn sources

  • Placing a position through NexID.
  • Generating a verified receipt.
  • Timing a narrative before major movement.
  • Ranking on EdgeBoards.
  • Sharing cards that bring users.
  • Minting .id and activating referral links.
  • Referring .id mints.
  • Reporting bad narrative-market matches.

Rewards and anti-gaming

Rewards should not pay raw volume alone. The engine must apply fee contribution, filled volume, realized winnings, unique-market breadth, verified receipts, active-day consistency, referral checks, suspicious pattern flags, and low-quality activity penalties. Lifetime points unlock the user's level; weekly score decides that week's allocation inside the user's level bucket.

Edge Points =
  Participation
+ Verified Receipt Reward
+ Timing Bonus
+ Board Movement Bonus
+ Referral Points
+ .id Boost
- Abuse Penalties
Weekly Reward Score =
  NexID Fee Contribution
+ Eligible Filled Volume
+ Realized Winnings
+ Unique Market Breadth
+ Verified Receipts
+ Active-Day Consistency
- Churn / Concentration Penalties

Blank card art prompts

Position entered card:
Create a premium blank 16:9 landscape crypto trading card background for a position-entry state. Dark luxury fintech style, black graphite background with subtle warm gold edge lighting, minimal abstract geometry, and one cinematic character on the right side. The character should feel like a modern CT trader avatar: focused expression, calm intensity, slightly forward posture, sharp silhouette, premium editorial lighting, not cartoonish, not anime, not childish. Leave the left side and lower third clean with generous negative space for future text overlays, metrics, logo, and position data. No text, no numbers, no symbols, no watermark, no logos.
Receipt/winning card:
Create a premium blank 16:9 landscape crypto receipt card background for a winning trade outcome. Dark graphite and black base, refined gold glow, subtle green performance accent, cinematic lighting, and one expressive character on the right side. The character should look confident and composed, with a restrained victorious expression — not shouting, not cartoonish, not meme-like. Leave the left side clean for large result text and metrics. Leave the bottom area clean for badges, proof labels, and referral/link overlays. No text, no numbers, no logos, no watermark.
Done/settled card:
Create a premium blank 16:9 landscape crypto card background for a completed/settled position. The mood should feel final, verified, and archival — like a legendary receipt being sealed into a reputation passport. Dark luxury background, black graphite texture, subtle gold rim light, clean geometric framing, and one calm character on the right side. Strong negative space on the left for final result text, identity, and proof data. No text, no numbers, no logos, no watermark.

13. .id passport

.id is the portable passport for edge. It carries receipts, board identity, referral links, reward account eligibility, verified profile state and future NexID access. It must never feel like a forced domain sale.

When .id appears

  • After a user places a position: soft prompt.
  • After a user generates a receipt: stronger prompt.
  • After a user ranks on a board: strongest prompt.

Unlocks

Verified profileReceipts and boards carry a memorable name.
Permanent archiveEdge history is preserved in the user profile.
Referral linkCards can earn when others mint .id.
Reward accountWeekly loyalty allocations require an active .id.
Official boards.id makes board identity official.
Enhanced cardsCustom card styles and passport cards.
Future accessNexID features, seasons and opportunity layers.
Trust anchor: .id does not buy reputation. It claims what the user earns.

Mint flow

  1. Search name
  2. Live rarity/price appears
  3. Available state
  4. Proceed to own name
  5. Select payment
  6. Reserve and confirm
  7. Activation state
  8. Success: .id is active
  9. Referral link appears
  10. Option to mint another .id

18. Admin tools

Admin tools must remain internal. They should never appear as public product menus.

Internal routes

  • /internal/rewards - reward cycle generation, allocation review, payout status and tx hash recording.
  • /internal/narrative-mapping — raw markets, category tags, mapping overrides, side mapping, quality status.
  • /internal/quality-review — no-trade states, suspicious markets, broken data.
  • /internal/receipts — receipt review, proof levels, dispute/appeal handling.
  • /internal/points — points ledger, anti-gaming flags, abuse actions.
  • /internal/referrals — referral attribution, pending/paid states, self-referral checks.

19. Risk, legal & privacy

The product should feel edgy, but risk language must exist in the right places: footer, order review, legal pages, restricted-location checks, and terms.

Required public/legal pages

Terms of UseAccounts, wallets, prohibited activity, product responsibilities.
Privacy PolicyWallets, positions, receipts, points, referrals, analytics, security signals.
Risk NoticeLoss risk, no financial advice, user responsibility.
Restricted LocationsEligibility and no bypassing restrictions.
Points and Rewards TermsPoints are game/season mechanics; rewards require eligibility review and abuse can remove points or block payout.
Referral Terms25% mechanics, fraud, payout timing, self-referrals.
.id Mint TermsMint finality, rarity pricing, no guaranteed resale value.
Reputation SafetyAppeals, wrong data, board abuse, impersonation.
API TermsFuture developer/API access conditions.

Privacy principle

Collect only what powers product outcomes: wallet identity, positions routed through NexID, receipts, points, referrals, optional display identity, and basic safety signals. Do not collect private keys, seed phrases, private DMs, full legal identity, or unnecessary wallet history in v1.

20. QA & edge states

V1 should not launch until these states are handled clearly.

Trading statesNot connected, wrong chain, restricted location, closed market, price moved, failed order, partial fill.
Data statesLoading, stale cache, broken market data, empty boards, no clean position available.
Card statesPNG export, copy link, share link, failed render, missing artwork, logo variant.
.id statesAvailable, unavailable, reserved, payment selected, minting, success, mint another, multiple names.
Dashboard statesNo positions, active positions, pending receipts, rewards pending/review/paid, referral locked, referral live.
AccessibilityKeyboard focus, escape-to-close, reduced motion, mobile sheets, readable contrast.

23. Launch plan

Soft beta

  • Launch with 5–8 high-quality narratives.
  • Use limited public access and manual curation.
  • Get users to share position/receipt cards.
  • Make Global Points visible from day one.

CT messaging

CT finally has receipts.
Ride or fade the timeline.
Let the board judge.

What not to lead with

  • Do not lead with “decentralized reputation protocol.”
  • Do not lead with “buy .id.”
  • Do not over-explain points in the main UI.
  • Do not use “prediction markets” as public positioning.

24. Non-negotiables

1. The UI never explains architecture when action will do.Users care about Ride/Fade, not rails.
2. The card is the growth engine.If users do not want to post it, the loop weakens.
3. .id is earned ownership, not a toll.Never force .id before the user understands value.
4. Leaderboards must be legendary.They should feel like an arena, not a spreadsheet.
5. Points are fun in the UI, serious in the engine.Keep product copy exciting; keep anti-abuse robust.
6. Quality beats quantity.Only surface markets that fit the narrative and pass quality checks.
